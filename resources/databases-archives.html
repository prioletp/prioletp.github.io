Databases & Archives

📚

Key astronomical databases, data archives and catalogues for target selection, photometry, spectroscopy and observing logs.

Observational Archives

🗄️
ESO Science Archive
Access all raw and reduced ESO data — including VLTI/MATISSE, GRAVITY and PIONIER observations — through the main ESO archive portal.
↗ 🗄️
OiDB (JMMC)
The Optical Interferometry Database — a searchable archive of published and pipeline-reduced interferometric measurements in OIFITS format.
↗ 🗄️
MAST (STScI)
Mikulski Archive for Space Telescopes — data from HST, JWST, TESS, Kepler and many other space missions.
↗

Catalogues & Databases

⭐
SIMBAD
The reference database for astronomical objects — identifiers, coordinates, bibliographic references and basic measurements for millions of objects.
↗ 📋
VizieR
Access to over 20,000 astronomical catalogues — photometry, spectral types, proper motions, and published data tables.
↗ 🪐
NASA Exoplanet Archive
NASA's database of confirmed exoplanets and host-star properties — essential for target selection and contextualising debris disk observations.
↗ 🛰️
Gaia DR3
ESA's Gaia mission data release 3 — precise parallaxes, proper motions, photometry and spectroscopy for nearly 2 billion stars.
↗

SED & Photometry

📈
VizieR SED Tool
Build spectral energy distributions from all available photometric catalogues in VizieR — quick multi-wavelength overview of any target.
↗ 📈
IRSA (NASA/IPAC)
Infrared Science Archive — data from WISE, Spitzer, 2MASS, IRAS and other infrared surveys, crucial for debris disk studies.
