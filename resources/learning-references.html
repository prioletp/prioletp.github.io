Learning & References

🎓

Textbooks, lecture notes, summer schools and key review articles for learning about optical interferometry, debris disks and related topics.

Textbooks

📖
Principles of Stellar Interferometry — A. Glindemann
Comprehensive textbook covering the fundamentals of optical interferometry — from coherence theory to practical instrumentation at the VLTI.
↗ 📖
An Introduction to Optical Stellar Interferometry — A. Labeyrie, S. G. Lipson, P. Nisenson
A thorough introduction to the field from historical context to modern techniques, with emphasis on aperture synthesis and imaging.
↗ 📖
Interferometry and Synthesis in Radio Astronomy — Thompson, Moran & Swenson
The classic reference on interferometry — although focused on radio, the Fourier theory and calibration concepts apply directly to optical work.
↗

Key Review Articles

📝
Debris Disks: Structure, Composition, and Variability — Wyatt (2008)
Authoritative review of debris disk physics — collisional cascades, dust dynamics, and the connection to planetary system architecture.
↗ 📝
Observations of Debris Disks — Matthews et al. (2014, PPVI)
Review from Protostars and Planets VI on observational techniques and results for debris disks across all wavelengths.
↗ 📝
Exozodiacal Dust — Kral et al. (review)
Review on hot and warm exozodiacal dust detection, possible origins (cometary activity, asteroid belts) and implications for exoplanet imaging.
↗

Schools & Lecture Notes

🎓
VLTI School
ESO's biennial VLTI school — lectures, hands-on tutorials and exercises covering interferometric techniques, data reduction and science cases.
↗ 🎓
Optical Interferometry Summer School (Liège)
European summer school on optical interferometry — theoretical foundations, practical sessions and group projects.
